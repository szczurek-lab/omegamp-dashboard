## LPS Binding -- BC Displacement Assay

This assay measures peptide binding to lipopolysaccharide (LPS) through competitive displacement of BODIPY-TR-cadaverine (BC), a fluorescent probe that associates with LPS. When a peptide binds LPS, it competes with and displaces BC, recovering the fluorescence signal. Higher BC-displacement values therefore indicate stronger competition for LPS binding.

### Normalization

- **0% displacement** = buffer + LPS + BC (reference state: BC fully bound to LPS)
- **100% displacement** = BC free in buffer, without LPS (maximum fluorescence of unbound BC)

The signal after peptide addition is compared against these two reference points. Reported BC-displacement (%) reflects where each peptide falls between the fully LPS-bound and free BC states.

### Controls

Polymyxin B is included as a positive control, given its well-characterized LPS-binding activity. It serves as a reference benchmark for peptide behavior, though it should not necessarily be interpreted as an absolute maximum binder under these assay conditions.

### Reading the dashboard

Four conditions are shown per peptide: three peptide concentrations without calcium (2, 8, 32 uM) and one with calcium (32 uM + Ca2+). Calcium can modulate LPS-peptide interactions by competing for the phosphate binding sites on lipid A, so calcium-tolerant binding may indicate a distinct binding mode.
